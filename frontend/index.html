Instructions

Tooth number. Clicking the checkbox marks the tooth as missing.

Implant. Clicking the checkbox replaces the tooth with an implant.

Mobility. Measured using both ends of the instrument handle. Enter values from 0 to 3.
The values represent:
0 · Mobility less than 0.5 mm.
1 · Mobility less than 1 mm.
2 · Mobility between 1 mm and 2 mm.
3 · Mobility greater than 2 mm and/or vertical mobility of the tooth.

Individual prognosis. Select: Good, Questionable, Poor, Hopeless.

Furcation. Measured with the curved Nabers probe.
Initial furcation: probe enters less than 3 mm. Marked by clicking once.
Moderate furcation: probe enters between 3 mm and 5 mm. Marked by clicking a second time.
Severe furcation: probe enters more than 5 mm or passes through the furcation. Marked by clicking a third time.

Bleeding.
Grade 1: Click once.
Grade 2: Click a second time.
Grade 3: Click a third time.
An average of bleeding scores is calculated and shown at the bottom of the periodontal chart.

Suppuration.
If there is suppuration, activate it with one click. Deactivate by clicking again.

Plaque. Activate to calculate the average plaque index based on 6 points per tooth. The result is displayed at the bottom of the periodontal chart.

Gingival width. The value noted is the band of gingiva from the gingival margin to the mucogingival junction on the vestibular sides of the teeth. Values from 0 to 2 mm are highlighted in red.

Gingival margin. The value is the distance from the CEJ (cementoenamel junction) to the gingival margin. Adding a minus sign before the value indicates it is located on the crown. Displayed in the chart with a blue band. Indicates gingival recession or hypertrophy (negative value).

Probing depth. The value is the distance from the gingival margin to the bottom of the pocket. Displayed in the chart with a red band. Pathological values (greater than 3 mm) are highlighted in red. The average is shown at the bottom of the periodontal chart, calculated only if values are entered for all teeth or implants.

Note. Free-text field for additional comments.

Average attachment level. Automatically shown at the bottom of the periodontal chart. It represents the average attachment loss, calculated as the sum of gingival margin and probing depth values.
